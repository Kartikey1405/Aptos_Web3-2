Museums

National Gallery of Modern Art, New Delhi


The National Gallery of Modern Art (NGMA) in New Delhi is a premier art museum showcasing India's rich modern art heritage. Established in 1954, it houses an extensive collection of works by prominent Indian artists, covering various genres and styles, from colonial to contemporary art, making it a vital center for art lovers and researchers alike.

Salar Jung Museum, Hyderabad


The Salar Jung Museum, located in Hyderabad, is one of India's largest museums, famous for its diverse collection of art, artifacts, and antiquities. It was founded by Nawab Mir Yousuf Ali Khan, commonly known as Salar Jung III. The museum features a vast array of exhibits, including sculptures, paintings, textiles, and rare manuscripts from around the world.

Victoria Memorial Hall, Kolkata


The Victoria Memorial in Kolkata is a grand white marble structure built in memory of Queen Victoria. Completed in 1921, it now serves as a museum and tourist attraction. The museum houses an extensive collection of British Raj-era artifacts, paintings, and historical manuscripts, offering insight into India's colonial history and architectural grandeur.

Allahabad Museum, Prayagraj


The Allahabad Museum, situated in Prayagraj, is known for its rich collection of ancient sculptures, terracotta artifacts, paintings, and memorabilia from India's freedom struggle. Established in 1931, it showcases the region's cultural heritage, featuring prehistoric artifacts, rare Buddhist and Jain relics, and works by famous Indian artists, including the renowned painter Nicholas Roerich.

Indian Museum, Kolkata


Established in 1814, the Indian Museum in Kolkata is the oldest and one of the largest museums in India. It houses an extensive collection of artifacts from various fields, including archaeology, geology, anthropology, and art. Notable exhibits include ancient sculptures, rare coins, fossils, and a rich display of textiles and crafts, reflecting India's diverse cultural heritage.
